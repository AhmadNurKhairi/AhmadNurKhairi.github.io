Case Studies

These are the case studies of organizations that migrated to Cloud successfully as well as the case studies where organization failed to adopt Cloud infrastructure.

Case study 1 - Humongous Big Data

Big Data may be too humble a word for the ambitious collaboration between SunGard Financial and Google to architect and construct a cloud system prototype that can store six years of U.S. inventory and other financial trading information — including each quote and trade — for regulators and inventory traders assessment. According to SunGard, that’s 100 billion events daily amounting to 30 petabytes over six years. The prototype is being created to compete for a U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission contract, called the Consolidate Auditing Trail (CAT). SunGard produced a prototype that could handle 10 billion events per hour. Obviously, the hardware, scalability and operational requirements were simply too big for SunGard to handle everything at home. Obviously, the hardware, scalability and operational requirements were merely too big for SunGard to manage everything at home. Amazingly, thanks to the time-to-market wonders of the Google Cloud, SunGard was able to construct a prototype in six weeks. Neil Palmer, SunGard's Chief Technology Officer for the exercise of consulting services, said, "There is no way we could have accomplished that, even with comparable techniques, if we had to stand up for our own infrastructure."

Case study 2 - Going All In with AWS

2nd Watch, A cloud-based business specializes in assisting other organisations move to AWS using its instruments and services to automate migration, procurement, procurement, activities, financial management and governance. 2nd Watch offers a wide range of advantages from its AWS-based solution, including time-to-market shortening, inner effectiveness, scalability, and the capacity to implement a comparable set of products and features worldwide. The AWS solution allowed the business to boost bookings year-over-year by a whopping 260 percent. Said Jeff Aden, EVP of 2nd Watch strategic business development and co-founder, “Working with AWS has specifically helped our product development team by allowing them to test and make changes to products quickly and inexpensively. It also helps us surpass our customers’ expectations with cost optimization and managed services, as several of our core products focus on those areas.”

Case study 3 - Failures associated with consolidation and virtualization

A curious tale about a UK company has been shared by Gary Barnett, chief analyst at Ovum. They originally believed they had 700 servers. When they started the consolidation project, they discovered that they actually owned 1,100 physical servers and ended up with 1,300 physical servers along with 2,000 virtual servers after the 18-month consolidation project was completed.The worst portion – this configuration of IT infrastructure had little effect on the quality of the facilities or the efficiency of the team.

Case study 4 - Failing to test everything before deploying

A financial service firm, Knight Capital, has implemented a new cloud-based high-frequency trading software in 2012, which began making unprofitable trades up to 40 times per second. As a consequence, all trade had to be stopped by the New York Stock Exchange. As a consequence, the business lost more than $440 million.
